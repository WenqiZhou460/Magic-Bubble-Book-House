The Very Hungry Caterpillar

By Eric Carle

A cute caterpillar was born. He was very hungry. He ate 1 apple, 2 pears, 3 plums, 4 strawberries, 5 oranges, cake, ice cream, pickles, etc. What would happen to him in the end?

×

Brown Bear, Brown Bear, What Do You See? Slide and Find

By Bill Martin Jr and Eric Carle

A trivia question about animals and colors: many small animals look at each other. A red bird is looking at a brown bear, and a yellow duck is looking at a red bird.

×

A Fish Out of Water

By Helen Palmer and P.D. Eastman

A boy went to a pet store and bought a little goldfish named Otto. The owner of the pet store told the boy: "Don't feed it too much." When the boy returned home, he looked at the hungry goldfish and fed it all the fish food. The goldfish Otto gradually grew bigger and bigger.

×

The Poky Little Puppy

By Janette Sebring Lowrey and Gustaf Tenggren

Five puppies sneak through a hole and venture into the grass and hillside. One of the puppies is missing, and it turns out that the slow pup is behind, slowly exploring the surrounding scenery.

×

The Giving Tree

By Shel Silverstein

A tree liked a boy very much. It liked the boy to play with it, let him climb up its branches, and eat its fruits. As the boy grew up, he needed more and more things, such as money, a house, and a boat.

Welcome to this website

This website introduces many books suitable for children to read. The books are divided into three categories: In the educational column, children can learn words, and recognize animals, colors, etc. from those picture books. In the emotional column, children can learn more about picture books related to emotions and moods. In the fantasy column, children can enter the fantasy adventure of the picture books.
